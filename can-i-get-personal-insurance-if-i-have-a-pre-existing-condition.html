Are there any special conditions for buying health insurance if you have a pre-existing condition?

Depending on your state, you may have to meet certain requirements to purchase health insurance. Many states require you to purchase a health insurance plan that is compliant with the “essential health benefits” outlined in the Affordable Care Act (ACA). The ACA also has a pre-existing condition waiver program that allows individuals with pre-existing conditions to purchase coverage from a state’s insurance exchange if they cannot find insurance that covers their pre-existing condition.

Should you buy a catastrophic health insurance plan?

A catastrophic health insurance plan is designed for those who have a high health risk. If you have a pre-existing condition, you may not be able to find affordable health insurance that covers your pre-existing condition. In this case, you may want to consider a catastrophic health insurance plan. These plans have higher premiums, but they do not have any pre-existing condition exclusions. They are also not subject to the same health underwriting guidelines as other health insurance plans.
